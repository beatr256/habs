Gorilla Trekking in Uganda:
The Ultimate Adventure of a Lifetime

Step into the heart of the African rainforest and experience one of the planet’s most intimate wildlife encounters — face-to-face with the endangered mountain gorillas of Uganda.

Known as the Pearl of Africa, Uganda offers a rare blend of untamed wilderness, breathtaking scenery, and unforgettable adventure.

Why Uganda?

Uganda is home to over half of the world’s remaining mountain gorillas, making it the best place on earth for a true gorilla encounter. Trek through misty jungles alive with birdsong, follow fresh tracks with your ranger, and feel your pulse quicken as you come within meters of a silverback and his family.

What to Expect

Your day begins with a morning briefing before you venture deep into Bwindi Impenetrable Forest or Mgahinga Gorilla National Park.
The hike can take a few hours — winding through thick jungle, climbing muddy paths, and crossing streams — but every step brings you closer to one of the most moving wildlife experiences in existence.

When you finally meet the gorillas, you’ll spend a full hour quietly observing them in their natural world — watching mothers care for their young, playful juveniles climb trees, and the silverback keeping a protective eye on his troop.

It’s an encounter that words can’t capture — raw, emotional, and deeply humbling.

Gorilla Habituation Experience

Want to spend even more time with these incredible creatures?
Join a Gorilla Habituation Experience and stay up to four hours with a gorilla family as researchers and rangers guide you through their daily routines. A rare, behind-the-scenes look at the lives of these gentle giants.

 

When to Go

The best time for gorilla trekking is during the dry seasons (December–February and June–August) when the trails are easier to hike.
However, the rainy months (March–May, September–November) bring lush landscapes and fewer crowds — perfect for photographers.

 

Travel Requirements

A Gorilla Trekking Permit is required and must be booked in advance.
Permits are limited to protect the gorillas — so early booking is highly recommended.

About the Mountain Gorillas of Uganda

 

The mountain gorillas of Uganda are among the world’s most fascinating and endangered animals. Over half of the global population of these gentle giants lives in Uganda’s lush rainforests — mainly in Bwindi Impenetrable National Park and Mgahinga Gorilla National Park. These parks are world-famous for gorilla trekking safaris, where visitors can experience a once-in-a-lifetime encounter with a wild gorilla family in their natural habitat.

Mountain gorillas live in family groups led by a dominant silverback, surrounded by females and playful young ones. They share about 98% of their DNA with humans, which makes observing them an emotional and deeply personal experience. Every gorilla family has its own character — from calm and curious to lively and playful.

Unlike lowland gorillas, mountain gorillas cannot survive in captivity. That’s why Uganda’s gorilla trekking tours play such an important role in their protection. Each gorilla trekking permit helps fund conservation programs and supports the local communities living around Bwindi and Mgahinga.

Visiting the gorillas is more than a safari — it’s a chance to witness the beauty of Uganda’s wild nature, support sustainable tourism, and contribute directly to the preservation of one of the planet’s most iconic species.
